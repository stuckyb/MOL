Encyclopedia of Life
The Enyclopedia of Life produces species pages that aggregate and organize knowledge from multiple digital sources. We anticipate future partnerships between us, in which EOL displays range map products we produce, while Map of Life incorporates EOL products such as images into our application.
Global Biodiversity Information Facility
The Global Biodiversity Information Facility faciliates the sharing of species point occurrences across a global network of providers. GBIF has provided Map of Life with ~150 million records of veretebrate occurrences, in order for those records to be mapped and used in refining species distribution products.
International Union for Conservation of Nature

The International Union for Conservation of Nature develops several spatial data products. Their expert opinion range maps are a key data product for Map of Life, which IUCN has graciously agreed to share with us.

UNEP-WCMC
The World Conservation Monitoring Program provides the World Database of Protected Areas (WDPA) and a biological inventory or checklist of species located in those protected areas, which serves as another key data source in Map of Life.
Senckenberg Museum and BIK-F
The Senckenberg Gesellschaft für Naturforschung (SGN), the Biodiversität und Klima Forschungszentrum Frankfurt (BIK-F) and Map of Life are united in our mission to produce, document and publish species distribution information. The BIK-F/SGN partnership will enable us to develop a more advanced infrastructure that will directly address the needs of BIK-F/SGN and similar institutions.
GMBA
GMBA and Map of Life will explore mountain biodiversity together, focusing on "filters" such as ruggedness and thermal belts. The range of climatic conditions and topographies across mountains offers 'experiments by nature' for testing biodiversity theory. Map of Life will faciliate the e-mining of openly accessible, interconnected electronic databases for scientific biodiversity research.
